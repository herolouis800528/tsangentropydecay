1. Philosophic Foundations

The Entropy-Decay Theory begins not as a mathematical invention, but as a philosophical critique of time, space, and energy as human-centered constructs. It challenges the assumption that time is a fundamental physical dimension, and asks whether entropy — as irreversible decay — is the real driver of physical reality. This section explores the metaphysical motivation behind replacing time (t) with directional decay steps (τ).

2. Theoretical Structure

This section introduces the core theoretical components of the framework. At its center is the concept that entropy propagates as a triggered internal decay process — not a substance moving through space, but a resonance-based alignment of entropic states. Dimensionality is reinterpreted as 33 independent decay channels, from -1D (failure stillness) to 32D (collapse state). The theory also introduces τ-resonance, memory fields, and the Second Entropy Cascade as critical organizing principles.

3. Hypothesis

The central hypothesis of the Entropy-Decay Theory is that all physical behavior — including motion, field propagation, mass, and structure — emerges from entropy-triggered decay processes aligned along independent dimensional channels. Specifically, when an external entropy source (such as light or current) matches a material’s intrinsic decay step frequency (τ-resonance), it can trigger a release of stored entropic memory. This release may cause nonlinear effects, dimensional shifts, or second-cascade transitions not predicted by conventional physics.

This hypothesis departs from traditional models that assume continuity in spacetime or particle-object ontology. It proposes instead that all transmission (light, signal, current) occurs by resonance-triggered internal replication — not physical passage — meaning nothing "travels through" a medium in the classical sense.

4. Mathematical Model

The mathematical backbone of the theory is a reformation of Tsang’s Equation, integrating both wave and stochastic decay functions as simultaneous entropic operations. This section explores how traditional physics equations are reshaped by eliminating explicit dependence on t (time), and instead express behavior through τ (decay steps) and Ψ (entropy curvature intensity). Dimensional fields (e.g., 12D conductivity, 14D surface resistance) are introduced not as spatial volumes but as entropic constraints.

5. Experimental Applications

While the theory is conceptual, it opens the door to physical experiments — particularly using naturally entropic materials. Geological substances like India Red marble or red granite may contain trapped entropic memory fields that can be released via τ-resonant triggering. This section outlines early experimental proposals, including devices designed to stimulate second entropy cascades in controlled environments, and detect resonance behavior not explainable by electromagnetic models alone.
